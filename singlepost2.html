Interaction Design

- Interaction Design Association (IXDA) Definition
IXDA defined the structure and behavior of interactive system
- Five Essential Principles:
Consistent, Visible, Learnable, Predictable, Feedback
- Consistency:
1. Since people are sensitive to changes
people pay attention to locations first
e.g.
content module to hide/show functionality
this functionality design should be applied to every page to make a uniform design pattern (so people can learn that pattern every time they access a new page)
2. Components with different behaviors should have different appearances
same presentation for different functionality confused people
e.g. tabs for photo gallery
3. Changes in appearance and behavior can attract unwanted attention
(be detailed oriented in your design)
- Visibility
1. Cannot invite interaction and engage visitors with your design if they are not aware that the opportunity to interact exists (Discoverability should not involve luck or chance)
clearly tell people that there’s interaction
2. Avoid situations where visitors falsely believe that they have reached the end of content or of an experience
“false bottom”
to avoid, use “content hinting”
3. Signal the availability of interaction with visual indicators that invite people to click or touch
people are “click-happy”
* Buttons - makes it 3D
* ICON & imgs
* Textures
* Text styles
4. Games and “Easter Eggs”(hidden features or messages) are special situations and exceptions to the rule of visibility
exploring should not be the important part for users to complete a task
- Learnability
1. Interactions should be easy to learn AND easy to remember
2. Learnability is often equated with “ease of use”, but even interfaces that are easy to use may require learning.
intuitive = easy to learn
3. People learn behaviors from experiences across the web and devices, and even from real-world places and objects.
take advan of what people already knows
(perceived affordance)
4. Design should set accurate expectations about what will happen before the interaction has occurred
(people should be able to predict the outcomes)
5. Use previews to show expectations
( labels, icon, instructions,…)
should indicate
1) what to do
2) what will happen
3) where the visitor will go
4) how the interface will respond
- Feedback
feedback can provide infos about:
1) Location
2) status or progress
3) future events or possibilities
4) completion or closure
1. never interrupt user experience
2. allow undo to reverse actions
e.g. downloading file
give info of progress of downloading
give file description
give cancel button
3. Every interaction should produce a noticeable and understandable reaction
Progress indicator
e.g.
downloading: 42% left…
