ShapeCoach - mobile app design

Posted: Jan, 2015. |By: Shufan GE

Introduction:

ShapeCoach is one of my own project in practicing mobile app visual design. From a purpose to design a better app to assist daily weight control or fitness plan, along with the experience and familarity in losing weight with mobile apps in past one year, I came up with the idea to devise an app that better assists those who want better body shape but lack of professional instructions.

Inspiration and research:

From using the app to accomplish my weight loss goal, I've tried different apps. From my observation, the on-market apps in healthiness and fitness can be categorized into the following four functionalities: diet control, workout recording, professional instructions, social circle for groups of people with same goals to share and communicate. As a non-professional people, I found the tools offered by app are useful, such as calorie calculator, and body fitness test to give me quantified standard to follow with, on the other hand, the unorganized, scattered functionality of different apps confuses me a lot. So I would combine the advantages to create an app with professionally organized functionalities to be utilized.

So I went ahead and ran a deep research on how those apps I found organized and useful. Here's some notes I took during the research, along with basic idea sketch of how my app should look like.

In my app, the functionality will be divided into four categories: 1) Diet Log; 2)Workout Log; 3)Sign in (can have professional coaching service purchased) or Guest free trial; 4) Social functions such as form communities to chat and sharing, as well as coach instructing a student group.

Information architecture:

To transferring the main functionalities into a real structure. I first sketch an information architecture documentation to start with. Here's the infromation architecture chart documentation.

Basic UI page sketch:

While building up the information architecture sketch, I gradually formed a vision how the app pages should look like, and with reference to the research results and the IA doc, I start sketching some of the pages of performing main functionalities.

which incorporate the four main functionalities in the bottom navigation bar that can always show the main four categories of tools that user can access. The page mainly consists of:

  • Bottom Navigation bar
  • Main page, where the main functionalities is performed
  • User personal account items organized in a drawer, hidden but shown when press the left-upper corner user portrait button
